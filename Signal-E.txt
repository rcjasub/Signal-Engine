The core idea is to build an Event-Driven Signal Engine.
Instead of a basic "app," you are building a high-speed filter that sits between a "firehose" of raw data and the final user. This architecture is the "holy grail" for both LinkedIn (processing social feeds) and Morgan Stanley (processing market trades).
The Workflow (The "Why it matters")
The Firehose (Producer): A Java service simulates 10,000+ events per second (e.g., Stock price changes or LinkedIn post views).
The Brain (Kafka Streams): This is your masterpiece. It looks at the stream and detects patterns in real-time.
Morgan Stanley angle: "Tell me if this stock price dropped 2% in under 60 seconds."
LinkedIn angle: "Tell me if this post just got 100 likes in the last 5 minutes (it’s trending)."
The Action (Consumer): A Java service that takes that "signal" and sends a notification or triggers an automated trade/alert.
Why this wins for both companies:
For Morgan Stanley: It shows you can handle financial volatility and building "low-latency" systems where every millisecond counts.
For LinkedIn: It shows you understand Social Graph dynamics—how to process massive user activity without breaking the database.
The "Secret Sauce" for your Resume:
By building your BullMQ clone in Go as a separate project, you can tell recruiters:
"I built the infrastructure (Go Task Queue) to understand how job scheduling works at the OS level, and then I built the platform (Java/Kafka Engine) to show how I solve high-scale business problems."
